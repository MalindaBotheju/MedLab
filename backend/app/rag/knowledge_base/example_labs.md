Elevated ALT/AST (liver enzymes) can be associated with hepatocellular injury.
Common associated causes include viral hepatitis, fatty liver disease,
medication effect, or alcohol use. Degree of elevation and pattern (ALT vs AST
predominant) both matter for interpretation and should be correlated with
clinical history.

Low hemoglobin (anemia) may be associated with iron deficiency, chronic
disease, blood loss, or bone marrow disorders. Mean corpuscular volume (MCV)
helps classify the type of anemia (microcytic, normocytic, macrocytic) and
guides further workup.

Elevated creatinine may be associated with reduced kidney function. Acute vs
chronic elevation is distinguished by trend over time and by clinical context
(e.g. dehydration, nephrotoxic medication exposure, chronic kidney disease).

Elevated troponin is a time-sensitive finding and may be associated with
myocardial injury. Any elevated troponin in the context of chest pain or
cardiac symptoms warrants urgent clinical correlation.
